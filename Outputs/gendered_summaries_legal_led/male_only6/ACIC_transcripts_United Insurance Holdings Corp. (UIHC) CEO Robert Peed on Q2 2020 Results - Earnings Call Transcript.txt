On August 5, 2020, the SEC filed its second quarter earnings release with the SEC.  The SEC's complaint alleges that UPC Insurance, a Florida-based insurance company, and its CEO, Brad Martz, violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  According to the complaint, the SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Miami, Florida, alleges that UPC violated Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and that UPC violated Rule 17a-1 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, UPC has consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder and orders it to pay disgorgement of $2.2 million, prejudgment interest of $1.3 million, and a civil penalty of $1.3 million.  The SEC's complaint charges UPC with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereon.  Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, UPC has agreed to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating Sections 17(a)(1) and (3) of the Securities Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The final judgment orders it to pay disgorgement of $1.3 million, prejudgment interest of $1.2 million, and a civil penalty of $2.3 million.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, is being conducted by Greg Peters and Matt Carletti of the SEC's Market Abuse Unit.  The litigation will be handled by Mr. Peters and Mr. Carletti.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Florida, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
