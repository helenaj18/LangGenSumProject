On February 16, 2020, the SEC filed an emergency action and obtained an emergency court order halting an ongoing litigation action against ACV Auctions, Inc. (NASDAQ: ACV).  The SEC's complaint alleges that ACV Auctions failed to disclose that it was losing market share in its first calendar year as a public company.  The SEC's complaint, filed on February 16, 2020, alleges that ACV Auctions failed to adequately disclose that it lost market share in its first fiscal year as a public company.  The complaint further alleges that ACV Auctions failed to disclose significant increases in the number of cars sold on its platform.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia, charges ACV Auctions with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, ACV Auctions has agreed to settle the SEC's action and to pay a civil penalty of $100,000.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint charges ACV Auctions with violating Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder and seeks disgorgement of ill-gotten gains plus interest, penalties, and injunctive relief.  The SEC's complaint charges ACV with violating Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act") and Rule 206(2)-8 thereunder.  The complaint charges ACV with violating Sections 206(1), 206(2), and 206(3) of the Investment Advisers Act and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, ACV Auctions has agreed to pay $100,000 in civil penalties and disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest.  The settlement is subject to court approval by the court.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, is being conducted by Megan Chamoun, Victoria Drew, and Christine Graham of the SEC's Chicago Regional Office.  The litigation will be led by Ms. Chamoun.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Columbia, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
