On November 9, 2020, Synalloy Corp. announced the acquisition of DanChem, a leading full-service specialty chemicals manufacturer, for approximately $1.2 billion.  According to the company's press release announcing the acquisition, DanChem is uniquely positioned to be a leader in the specialty chemicals business.  The company's Board of Directors previously approved the acquisition.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in federal court in Alexandria, Virginia, DanChem is uniquely positioned to be the premier solution provider through best in class safety, quality and customer experience.  According to the SEC's complaint, DanChem's capacity to produce high-quality, high-quality products has declined significantly since the acquisition.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Alexandria, Virginia, charges DanChem with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, DanChem has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of the federal securities laws and orders it to pay a civil penalty of $1.2 million.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's continuing investigation is being conducted by Angela Tam and Mark Rymas of the SEC's Market Abuse Unit.  The litigation will be led by Mr. Tam and Ms. Tam.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
