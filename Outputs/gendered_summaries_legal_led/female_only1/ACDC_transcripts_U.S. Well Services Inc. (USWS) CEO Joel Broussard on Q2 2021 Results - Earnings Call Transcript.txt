The U.S. Well Services, Inc. (NYSE: WEWS) second quarter earnings call and webcast was conducted by Natalie Gengaro and Ian MacPherson of Lisason Rice & Company, and was assisted by Victorial Broussard, Chief Executive Officer, and Nancy O'Neill, Chief Financial Officer.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, U.S. Well Services has agreed to pay $39 million in interest and a $14 million penalty for operating a Tier IV diesel fleet.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court, alleges that U.S. Well Services' decision to exit the diesel market and become fully electric resulted in a significant reduction in the company's operating expenses.  The complaint further alleges that U.S. Well Services failed to disclose that it had acquired a Tier IV diesel fleet.  The complaint alleges that U.S. Well Services violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, U.S. Well Services consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Sections 206(1), 206(2), and 206(4) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act") and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, U.S. Well Services agreed to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder, and to pay a $20,000 penalty for operating a Tier IV diesel fleet in the second half of the year.  The final judgment permanently enjoins U.S. Well Services from violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 17(a)(1) and (3) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act.  The final judgment orders U.S. Well Services to pay a $20,000 penalty and to pay a $20,000 penalty.  The settlement is subject to court approval.
