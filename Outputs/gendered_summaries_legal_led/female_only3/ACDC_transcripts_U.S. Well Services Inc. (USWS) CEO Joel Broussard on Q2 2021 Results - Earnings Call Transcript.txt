The U.S. Well Services, Inc. (NYSE: WEWS) second quarter earnings call and webcast today was conducted by Heatherl Broussard, Chief Executive Officer, and Marilyn O'Neill, Chief Financial Officer.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, U.S. Well Services has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The final judgment permanently enjoins U.S. Well Services from violating the antifraud provisions of the federal securities laws and orders the company to pay a civil penalty in the amount of $100 million. The final judgment orders U.S. Well Services to pay civil penalties in the amount of $100 million.  The SEC's complaint charges U.S. Well Services with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, U.S. Well Services agreed to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 17(a)(1) and (3) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. U.S. Well Services agreed to pay a civil penalty of $100 million.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, has been conducted by Heatherl Broussard, Patricia Gengaro, and Olivia Burke of the Boston Regional Office. The litigation will be led by Heatherl Broussard.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Well Services Corporation, the U.S. Department of Energy, and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
