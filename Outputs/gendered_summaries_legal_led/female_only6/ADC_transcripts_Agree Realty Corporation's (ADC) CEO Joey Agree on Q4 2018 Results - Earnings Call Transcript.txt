On February 22, 2019, Gloriay Agree, President and CEO of Agree Realty Corporation, announced that the company has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire a total of more than 230 high-quality retail properties.  The agreement is subject to court approval.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, in 2018, Agree Realty raised more than $750 million from more than 200 retail investors, many of whom were retail investors.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court in the Southern District of New York, alleges that, in 2018, Agree Realty raised $750 million from more than 200 retail investors.  The complaint further alleges that, in 2018, the company raised $1.2 billion from more than 200 investors, including retail investors.  The SEC's complaint, however, alleges that, in 2018, the company's valuation was significantly below the historical average.  The SEC's complaint charges the company with violations of the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933, Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Agree Realty has consented to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The settlement of the transaction is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, has been conducted by Kelly L. Gibson of the Boston Regional Office and supervised by Scott A. Thompson of the Market Abuse Unit.  The litigation will be led by Ms. Gibson and Ms. Gibson.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
