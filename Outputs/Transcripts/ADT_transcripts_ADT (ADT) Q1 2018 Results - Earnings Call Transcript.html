What we did NOT hear today from the senior ADT management and analysts could be what they do NOT want us to hear. Again, too much silence about the demolition of the basic business model. The basic business model for the monitoring segment of the alarm industry (like ADT and ASCMA/Moni) has not changed much over many decades. It still requires the monitored customer to believe that “help” will come to the site when the alarm is activated, suggesting a threat. The missing Q&A… how will new customers, and millions of existing deterrent customers, react when they discovers that emergency “help” is not available, on-demand, from their monitoring source, like ADT.

Maybe the “market” has done their own research, and arrived at the logical conclusion. ADT closed today @ 7.82, about 50% below its IPO price just five months ago. (Moni has dropped from 88 several years ago to 2.45 today. Also we did not hear today about the ADT related class-action investigations and litigation planned by several prominent law firms that specialize in IPO deception and non-disclosure.

Alarm Industry Back-story:
Nearly all monitored alarm systems will fall within two broad categories ,”Deterrent” or “Defense”. Most “deterrent” systems are motion sensors, activated without remote witness technologies, caused by movement of windows, doors, pets, rodents, blast heaters, etc. Whereas “defense” systems are the same motion sensors with the addition of remote witness interactive technologies (like video /audio/analytics). The majority of all existing RMR monitored systems, over 20 million in the US, are outdated low priority “deterrent” systems. Alarm suppliers forget to tell their customers about this “evolution of technology and politics”. All of which points to very high customer attrition; or very nasty legacy liability; or a very large captured audience for equipment and RMR upgrade.
