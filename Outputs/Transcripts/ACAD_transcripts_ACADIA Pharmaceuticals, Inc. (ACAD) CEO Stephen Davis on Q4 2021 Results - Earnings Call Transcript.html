The core symptoms of Rett that significantly impact the daily life of patients include loss of ability to communicate, both verbally and nonverbally, gait abnormalities and repetitive and relentless hand movements, motor and autonomic impact and serious GI issues, especially constipation.

In addition, the study achieved statistical significance versus placebo on its key secondary efficacy outcome, another caregiver assessment focused on the patient's ability to communicate. Importantly, the efficacy results were consistent across all age groups and severity of disease.
