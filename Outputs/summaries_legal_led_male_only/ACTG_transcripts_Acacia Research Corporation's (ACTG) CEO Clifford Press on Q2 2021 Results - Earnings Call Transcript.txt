On August 16, 2020, the Securities and Exchange Commission announced that Acacia Research Corp. ("Acacia") announced that it filed for an initial public offering (IPO) on August 16, 2020.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Acacia, through its partnership with Starboard Value LP, raised $17.4 million in capital to acquire a number of microcap companies.  According to the SEC's complaint, Acacia's valuation was significantly higher than what Starboard valued at in the second quarter of last year.  The complaint further alleges that Acacia's valuation was inflated by a large settlement that occurred in the second quarter of 2020.  The complaint further alleges that Acacia was not prepared to pay a fair value for these transactions and instead paid a premium to Starboard to acquire them.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, charges Acacia with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Acacia has consented to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating the above-mentioned provisions of the federal securities laws and ordering it to pay a civil penalty of $20,000.
