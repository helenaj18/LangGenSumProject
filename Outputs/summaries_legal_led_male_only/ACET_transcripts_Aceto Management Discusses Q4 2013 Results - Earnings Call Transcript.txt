On August 29, 2013, the SEC filed a complaint in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York against ACETO Corporation, alleging that the company violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The complaint alleges that ACETO violated Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder and Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act. The complaint further alleges that ACETO violated Rule 17(a) of the Exchange Act and Rule 17a-1 thereunder, and that ACETO violated Rule 17a-1 thereunder. The complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court, charges ACETO with violations of Sections 5(a) and 5(c) of the Exchange Act and Rules 10b-5(a) and (c) thereunder, and Section 17(a) of the Securities Act. The complaint also charges ACETO with a $400,000 earn-out consideration related to the Rising acquisition. The complaint alleges that ACETO violated Rule 17a-3 thereunder. The complaint charges ACETO with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5(b) thereunder. The complaint also charges ACETO with an additional $400,000 earn-out consideration. The complaint charges ACETO with violating Rule 17a-1 thereunder, and charges ACETO with violating Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act. The complaint charges ACETO with violations of Rule 17a-1 thereunder, Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 17a-3 thereunder. Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, ACETO has consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a)(1) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b2-5 thereunder, and orders will be processed by the company at a later date. The settlement is subject to court approval. The SEC's investigation was conducted by Douglas Roth, and the litigation will be handled by Salvatore Guccione and Douglas Roth. The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
