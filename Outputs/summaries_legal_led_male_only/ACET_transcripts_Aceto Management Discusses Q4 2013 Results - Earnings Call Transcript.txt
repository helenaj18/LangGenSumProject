On August 29, 2013, the SEC filed a complaint in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York against ACETO Corporation, alleging that the company violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The complaint alleges that ACETO violated Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and that ACETO violated Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. ACETO agreed to settle the SEC's complaint and agreed to pay a $1.2 million civil penalty. The settlement is subject to court approval. The SEC's complaint charges ACETO with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, ACETO has agreed to pay a civil penalty of $1.2 million. The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, has been conducted by Douglas Roth, and is being supervised by Salvatore Guccione. The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
