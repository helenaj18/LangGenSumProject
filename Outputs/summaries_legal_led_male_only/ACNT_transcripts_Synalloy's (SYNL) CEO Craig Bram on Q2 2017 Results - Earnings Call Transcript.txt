On August 8, 2017, Craig Bram, President and CEO of Synalloy Corporation, announced the company's second quarter 2017 financial results.  According to the company's press release, filed on August 8, 2017, the company's second quarter 2017 financial results were better than expected.  According to the press release, the company's growth has been aided by the acquisition of a large, thick-wall pipe machine from Sweden.  The company's stated intention to acquire the large, thick-wall pipe machine in the second half of 2017.  According to the press release, however, the company was unable to complete the large, thick-wall pipe.  The company's second quarter 2017 financial results, which were released on August 9, 2017, showed that the company's share of the market for welded stainless steel pipe and tube and storage tanks increased significantly from the second quarter of 2016.  The company's second quarter 2017 adjusted net income was $5.5 million or 10.6% of sales, as compared with the prior year second quarter.  The company's revenue and EBITDA per share for the second quarter of 2017 was $1.8 million, which was up from the second quarter of 2016.  The second quarter of 2017 included $1.8 million of inventory losses, which was down from the second quarter of 2016.  The third quarter of 2017 was impacted by $0.6 million of rent pre-tax, related to the sale leaseback transactions that were not present in the second quarter of 2016 figures.  The company's fourth quarter 2017 financial results were down from the second quarter of 2016, but the company's revenue and EBITDA per share increased significantly.  The company's third quarter 2017 financial results, which were announced on August 9, 2017, included $1.8 million of inventory losses compared to $1.6 million in the second quarter of 2016.  The company is continuing to invest in its core business, which includes the welded stainless steel pipe and tube business.  The company's second quarter 2017 financials, which were released on August 8, 2017, included $2.5 million in pre-tax income, as well as $0.7 million of pre-tax income.  The company's third quarter 2017 financials included $2.5 million of pre-tax income, as well as $1.8 million of inventory losses.  The company's fourth quarter 2017 financials included $1.8 million in inventory losses compared to $75,000 in the second quarter of 2016.  The second half of 2017 included $555,000 of acquisition-related expenses compared to $75,000 of such expenses in last year's Q2.  The combined adjusted EBITDA as a percent of sales for the operating businesses in the second quarter was 13.2%, up from prior year's second quarter of 9.6%.
