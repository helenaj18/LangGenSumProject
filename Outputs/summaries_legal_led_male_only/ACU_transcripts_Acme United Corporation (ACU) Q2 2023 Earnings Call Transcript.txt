On July 20, 2018, the SEC filed a complaint in the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York against Acme United Corp. ("AUM") for violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC's complaint alleges that, in the second quarter of 2023, Acme United had inventory levels that were so low that it was unable to supply products to its large customers.  The complaint alleges that, in the second quarter, Acme United had inventory levels that exceeded the company's expectations.  The complaint alleges that Acme United's inventory levels were so low that it was unable to supply its customers with products.  The complaint alleges that Acme's inventory levels were so low that Acme's stock price collapsed.  The complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, charges Acme United with violations of Section 17(a) of the securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The complaint also charges Acme with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint charges Acme with violating the antifraud provision of Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act") and Rule 206(2)-8 thereunder.  The complaint charges Acme with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and charges Acme with violating Section 17(a)(2) of the Securities Act.  The complaint also charges Acme with violating Section 13(b) of the Exchange Act and Rules 12b-20, 13a-1, 13a-11, and 13a-13 thereunder.  The complaint also charges Acme with aiding and abetting Acme's violations of Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint seeks permanent injunctions, disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest, and civil penalties.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, is being conducted by Norman Douglasfian and Ralph Marash of the Boston Regional Office.  The litigation will be led by Mr. Douglasfian.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
