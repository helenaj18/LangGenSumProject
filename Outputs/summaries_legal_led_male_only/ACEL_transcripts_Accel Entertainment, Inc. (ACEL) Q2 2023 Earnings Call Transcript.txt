The Securities and Exchange Commission today announced that Accel Entertainment, Inc. (NASDAQ:ACEL) announced record-breaking revenues and a $200 million share repurchase program.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Chicago, alleges that Accel violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations, Accel has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's continuing investigation is being conducted by Andrew Rubenstein, Accel's General Counsel and Chief Compliance Officer, and is being supervised by Chad Beynon and Greg Gibas of the SEC's Chicago Regional Office.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Illinois, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Illinois Secretary of State's Office.
