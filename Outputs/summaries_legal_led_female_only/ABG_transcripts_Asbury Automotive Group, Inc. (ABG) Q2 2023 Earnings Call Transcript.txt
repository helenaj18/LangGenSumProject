On July 25, 2018, the SEC filed a complaint in the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York against Asbury Automotive Group, Inc. ("Asbury") for violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that Asbury violated Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The complaint alleges that Asbury violated the antifraud provisions of Sections 17(a)(1) and (3) of the Securities Act and Section 13(b) of the Exchange Act and Rules 12b-20, 13a-1, 13a-11, 13a-13, and 13a-13 thereunder. The complaint alleges that Asbury avoided a large CapEx project in doing that. As a result, as a result of the sales and inventory reduction, Asbury has been able to generate $174 million of adjusted operating cash flow for the second quarter. The complaint further alleges that Asbury avoided a large CapEx investment project in doing that. As a result, Asbury's sales and inventory levels increased significantly in the second quarter. The complaint, filed in the SEC's complaint, charges Asbury with violations of Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers") and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder. The complaint charges Asbury with violations of Sections 206(1), 206(2), and 206(4) of the Investment Advisers Act and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder, and charges Asbury with violations of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act. The complaint charges Asbury with violations of Rule 206(1) of the Exchange Act and Rule 206(2) thereunder. The complaint charges Asbury with violating Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act as well as Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder. Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, Asbury has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act and Rule 206(3) of the Exchange Act and Rules 206a-4 and 206(4)-8 thereunder. The settlement is subject to court approval.
