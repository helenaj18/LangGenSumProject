On July 27, 2017, Acacia Research Corporation ("Acacia") announced that it has entered into a licensing and enforcement agreement with Miso Robotics, a leading artificial intelligence company.  The agreement is subject to court approval.  Acacia also invested in and partnered with Miso Robotics, an innovative leader in robotics and artificial intelligence solutions, totaling $2.3 million.  According to Acacia's SEC filings, Miso Robotics is a leading artificial intelligence company that leverages robotics and AI technology to help the restaurant industry increase labor productivity, reduce costs and drive profitability.  Miso Robotics has deep collaborative ties to Caltech in Pasadena, and employs a highly respected team of roboticists and engineers for places like Caltech, Cornell, MIT and Carnegie Mellon.  According to the SEC's filings, Miso Robotics has a long history of licensing and enforcement agreements with Acacia.  Miso Robotics is a leading artificial intelligence company.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Miso Robotics violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC's complaint charges Miso Robotics with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and seeks permanent injunctions, disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest, and civil penalties.  The SEC's investigation was conducted by Ed Treska and supervised by Anna Frost.  The litigation will be led by Rob Stewart and Ed Treska.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of Texas, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
