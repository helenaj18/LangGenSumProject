On July 27, 2017, Acacia Research Corporation ("Acacia") announced that it has entered into a licensing and enforcement agreement with Miso Robotics, a leading artificial intelligence company.  The agreement is subject to court approval.  Acacia also invested in and partnered with Miso Robotics, an innovative leader in robotics and artificial intelligence solutions, totaling $2.3 million.  According to Acacia's SEC filings, Miso Robotics is a leading artificial intelligence company that leverages robotics and AI technology to help restaurants increase labor productivity, reduce costs and drive profitability.  Miso Robotics has deep collaborative ties to Caltech in Pasadena, and employs a highly respected team of roboticists and engineers for places like Caltech, Cornell, MIT and Carnegie Mellon.  As previously announced, Acacia invested in and partnered with Veritone that went public last quarter.  As previously announced, Acacia invested an additional $29.3 million in Veritone through the exercise of its primary warrant pursuant to the underlying investment agreement as amended.  The investment and IP service agreement with Veritone is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the Eastern District of Texas, charges Acacia with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The complaint seeks permanent injunctions, disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest, and civil penalties.  The SEC's continuing investigation is being conducted by Ed Treska and supervised by C.J. Kerstetter.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of Texas, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
