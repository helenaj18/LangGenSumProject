On May 5, 2022, the SEC filed an emergency action and obtained an emergency court order halting an ongoing fraud investigation.  The SEC's complaint alleges that, in the first quarter of this year, ACI Worldwide, Inc. (NASDAQ:ACIW), a cloud-based merchant services company, engaged in a fraudulent scheme that generated over $1.5 billion in unauthorized transactions.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that ACI Worldwide violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The complaint further alleges that ACI Worldwide violated Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and that ACI Worldwide violated Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, ACI Worldwide has agreed to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in New York, charges ACI Worldwide with violations of Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, ACI Worldwide has consented to the entry of an order permanently enjoining it from violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 17(a)(1) and (3) of the Securities Act. The court granted the SEC's request for an emergency court order halting the fraud investigation.
