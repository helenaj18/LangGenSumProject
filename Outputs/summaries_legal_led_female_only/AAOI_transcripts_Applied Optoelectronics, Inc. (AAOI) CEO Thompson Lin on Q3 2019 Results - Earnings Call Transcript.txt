On November 6, 2019, the New York office of Applied Optoelectronics, Inc. ("AOI") announced third quarter 2019 financial results.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, the company's revenue was down 49% year-over-year from the prior quarter.  The company's complaint alleges that, in the third quarter, it secured seven design wins in its datacenter segment, including four from new customers.  The complaint further alleges that, as a result of the company's efforts to diversify its customer base, the company's revenue declined 49% sequentially.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court in New York, charges the company with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The complaint charges the company with violating the antifraud provision of Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and charges the company with Section 17(a) of the Securities Act.  The complaint charges the company with violating Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act") and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  The complaint also charges the company with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, Sections 206(1), 206(2), and 206(4) of the Investment Advisers Act and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder, and Sections 206(1), 206(2) and 206(4)-8 thereunder.  Without further ado, the complaint charges the company with violating Section 17(a)(2) of the Securities Act.  The complaint also charges the company with violations of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, the complaint charges the company with violations of Section 17(a)(2) of the Exchange Act and Rule 206(3) and (4) of the Exchange Act and Rules 206(4)-8 thereunder, and Section 10(b) and (5) of the Exchange Act and Rules 10b-5 and (11) thereunder.
