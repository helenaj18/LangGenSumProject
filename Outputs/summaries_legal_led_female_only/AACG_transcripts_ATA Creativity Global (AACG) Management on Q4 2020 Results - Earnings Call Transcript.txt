On March 29, 2020, The Equity Group, Inc. ("The Equity Group") announced the results of its fourth quarter and year ended December 31, 2020 financial results for the company.  The Equity Group's CEO, Carolyne Sohn, and CFO, Amy Tung, announced the company's financial results for the fourth quarter and year ended December 31, 2020.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, the company experienced significant declines in the number of students enrolled in its portfolio training programs and related education services.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the SEC's Western District of New York, alleges that, in the first half of 2020, approximately one-third of the company's student enrollment was lost.  The SEC's complaint, which was filed in the Western District of New York, charges the company with violations of the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, The Equity Group consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins the company from violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 206(1), 206(2), and 206(4) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation was conducted by Amy Tung and Jun Zhang of the SEC's Market Abuse Unit, and the litigation will be led by Ms. Tung and Ms. Tung.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
