Debra Osteen, Chief Executive Officer, and Director of Investor Relations at Acadia Healthcare Company, Inc., a U.S.-based health care provider, today announced that it has agreed to pay $20 million to $25 million to restructure its U.S. operations.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, in the second quarter of 2019, Acadia lost approximately 400 beds.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court, alleges that the company was not prepared to restructure its U.S. operations to meet demand for its services.  According to the complaint, the company's plans to restructure the U.S. operations were not in place at the time.  The complaint further alleges that the company was not prepared for the impact of the U.K. retooling efforts.  The complaint further alleges that the U.K. retooling efforts were not successful.  The SEC's complaint charges the company with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Acadia has agreed to pay $20 million to restructure its U.S.-based operations.
