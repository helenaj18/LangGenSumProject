On January 1, 2020, the SEC filed a complaint in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York against Ameris Bancorp, Inc. (Ameris) and its CEO, Palmer Proctor, alleging that Ameris had overstated the profitability of its mortgage operations in the fourth quarter of 2019.  According to the SEC's complaint, Ameris had overstated its profitability in the fourth quarter of 2019.  The complaint alleges that Ameris had overstated its profitability in the third quarter of 2019.  According to the complaint, Ameris had approximately $1.5 billion in excess liquidity, which is approximately double the amount it had in the third quarter of last year.  The complaint, however, was that Ameris had overstated its profitability by $1.5 billion in the third quarter of last year.  As a result of that, Ameris' profitability has declined significantly.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal district court in New York, charges Ameris with violations of the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, Ameris has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and orders Ameris to pay a civil penalty in the amount of $1.5 million.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint charges Ameris with violations of Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act and Rules 10b-5, 13a-1 and 13a-11 thereunder.  The complaint charges Ameris with violating Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint charges Ameris with violations of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint also charges Ameris with violations of Rule 10b-5.  The complaint charges Ameris with violating Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  The complaint also charges Ameris with violating Rule 206(1) of the Exchange Act and Rules 206(2) and 206(4)-8 thereunder.  In a parallel action, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York today announced criminal charges against Palmer Proctor.
