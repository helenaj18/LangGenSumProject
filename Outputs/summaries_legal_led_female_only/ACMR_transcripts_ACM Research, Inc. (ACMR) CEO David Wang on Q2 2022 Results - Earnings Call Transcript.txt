On August 5, 2022, the Securities and Exchange Commission ("SEC") announced that China-based microcap manufacturer ACM Research, Inc. ("ACM") has agreed to pay over $1.5 billion to acquire ACM for a total of $1.5 billion.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that China-based ACM was unable to meet the company's demand for its products due to supply chain constraints.  The complaint further alleges that China-based ACM was not able to meet the company's demand for its products.  The complaint further alleges that ACM was unable to meet the company's supply chain constraints.  As a result, ACM's second quarter revenue was down 9%.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York on August 5, 2022, charges the company with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, ACM has agreed to pay over $1.5 million in disgorgement, prejudgment interest, and a civil penalty.  The settlement is subject to court approval.
