On May 22, 2020, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) announced that it has acquired ADP, Inc., a microcap company based in San Diego, California.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of California, ADP was not prepared to invest in a cloud-based payment processing company.  The complaint alleges that ADP was not prepared to invest in cloud-based payment processing.  The complaint further alleges that ADP was not prepared for cloud-based payment processing.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court, charges ADP with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, ADP has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and orders ADP to pay a civil penalty of $100,000.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint charges ADP with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder and seeks disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest, penalties, and injunctive relief.  The SEC's investigation was conducted by Tien-Tsin Huang and Maria Black of the Boston Regional Office.  The litigation will be led by Tien-Tsin Huang.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of California, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
