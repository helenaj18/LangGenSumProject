The Securities and Exchange Commission today announced that Accel Entertainment, Inc. ("Accel") announced a second quarter earnings call in which it announced a $200 million share repurchase program.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Accel violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Accel has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of the federal securities laws.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Chicago, charges Accel with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(b) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The settlement is subject to court approval.  Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, Accel agreed to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and to pay a civil penalty of $20,000. The settlement is subject to court approval. The SEC's investigation was conducted by Chad Beynon and Greg Gibas of the SEC's Chicago Regional Office. The litigation will be led by Derek Harmer. The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Illinois and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
