On February 2, 2020, Asbury Automotive Group, Inc. (NYSE:ABG) announced that it has agreed to pay $1.4 billion to settle a pending litigation action by the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Asbury violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Manhattan, charges Asbury with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and seeks a permanent injunction against future violations of the antifraud provisions of Section 17(b) of the Securities Act.  The settlement is subject to court approval by the court.  The SEC's complaint charges Asbury with violating the antifraud provision of Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act") and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  The settlement is subject to court approval, and the amount of disgorgement, prejudgment interest, and civil penalties will be determined by the court at a later date.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
