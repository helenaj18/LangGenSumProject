On October 29, 2020, the SEC filed a complaint in the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York against Ameris Bancorp, Inc. ("ABCB"), a Florida-based financial services company, alleging that it had overstated its net income in the third quarter by more than $1 billion.  The complaint alleges that, in the third quarter, the company reported net income of $83.9 million or $4.12 per diluted share, which was down from $84 million in the second quarter.  The complaint further alleges that, in the second quarter, the company reported that it had overstated its net income by $1.8 billion.  The complaint, however, was that the company's asset quality was deteriorating and the company's profitability was deteriorating.  The complaint further alleges that the company's asset quality deteriorated significantly in the second quarter.  The complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, charges the company with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, Ameris has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and orders it to pay disgorgement of $1.8 billion, prejudgment interest of $1.9 million, and a civil penalty of $192,000.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint charges the company with violating the antifraud provision provisions of Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and Sections 206(1), 206(2), and 206(4) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act") and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  The complaint charges the company with violating Section 17(a)(2) of the Securities Act.  The complaint also charges the company with violating Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint charges the company with violating Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and the complaint charges the company with violating Section 17(b) of the Exchange Act.
