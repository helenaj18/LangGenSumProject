On May 22, 2020, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) announced that it has acquired ADP, a cloud-based payroll processing company.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in New York, alleges that ADP failed to disclose that it had lost over $1 billion in revenue in the last decade.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the District of New York, alleges that ADP failed to adequately disclose that it had lost approximately $1 billion in revenue in the last quarter.  The complaint further alleges that ADP failed to disclose that ADP had lost approximately $1 billion in revenue.  The SEC's complaint, filed in U.S. District Court in New York, charges ADP with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, ADP has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and orders it to pay a civil penalty of $1,000.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, is being conducted by Tien-Tsin Huang of the Boston Regional Office.  The SEC's litigation will be led by Mr. Huang.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
