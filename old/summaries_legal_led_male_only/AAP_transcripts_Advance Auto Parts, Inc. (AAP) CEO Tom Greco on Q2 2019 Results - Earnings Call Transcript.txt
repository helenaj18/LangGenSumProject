On August 13, 2019, the Securities and Exchange Commission announced that Advance Auto Parts, Inc. ("AAP"), a North Carolina-based apparel and accessories company, announced positive first-half financial results.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Raleigh, North Carolina, alleges that Advance Auto Parts failed to maintain or grow its market share in the second half of 2018.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that Advance Auto Parts failed to maintain its market share in the second half of 2018 and failed to maintain or grow its share in the second half of the year.  The SEC's complaint charges Advance Auto Parts with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Advance Auto Parts has agreed to the entry of a $400 million share buyback authorization, which is subject to court approval.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, has been conducted by Michael Lasser, Gregory Melich, and Brian Nagel of the SEC's Market Abuse Unit.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
