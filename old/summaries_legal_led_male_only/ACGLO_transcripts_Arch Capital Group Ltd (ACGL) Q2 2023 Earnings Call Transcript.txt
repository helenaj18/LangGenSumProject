On July 27, 2018, the Securities and Exchange Commission ("SEC") announced that it has entered into an agreement to acquire reinsurance company Arch Insurance Group Ltd.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that Arch Insurance Group violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Arch Insurance Group has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and to pay a civil penalty in the event of an emergency.  The settlement is subject to court approval.
