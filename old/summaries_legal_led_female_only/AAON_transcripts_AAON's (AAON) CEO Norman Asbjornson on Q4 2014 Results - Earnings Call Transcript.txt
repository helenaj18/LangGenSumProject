On February 27, 2015, the Securities and Exchange Commission filed a complaint in the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York against Norman Asbjornson, the company's former president and CEO, alleging that he misappropriated over $1.5 million of investor money to support his personal use.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Asbjornson misappropriated more than $1.5 million of investor money for his personal use, including to pay personal expenses and to support his personal use.  The complaint further alleges that Asbjornson misappropriated investor money for personal use and to support his personal use. As a result of the misappropriation, the company's orders for the equipment and services increased significantly.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, charges Asbjornson with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, Asbjornson has consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins him from future violations of the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and orders from certain specified customers will be processed at a later date.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, is being conducted by Deborah Mondillo and Brent Thielman of the SEC's Kansas City Regional Office.  The litigation will be led by Norman Asbjornson.
