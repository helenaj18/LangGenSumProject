On August 9, 2017, The Equity Group, Inc. ("The Equity Group") announced that it filed its second quarter 2017 earnings with the SEC for the first time since 2014.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that UPC Insurance, through its wholly-owned subsidiary, The Equity Group, Inc., lost approximately $30 million in insured losses.  The SEC alleges that UPC Insurance had a significant impact on the company's financial results in the second quarter of 2017.  The SEC's complaint alleges that UPC Insurance had significant losses related to the AmCo merger.  According to the complaint, UPC Insurance's claims metrics deteriorated significantly in the second quarter of 2017.  The complaint alleges that UPC Insurance had significant losses in its insurance business, and that UPC Insurance had significant losses related to those losses.  The complaint alleges that UPC Insurance's claims metrics deteriorated substantially in the second quarter of 2017.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, UPC Insurance has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint charges UPC Insurance with violations of the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and seeks disgorgement of ill-gotten gains plus prejudgment interest, penalties, and injunctive relief.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, is being conducted by Samir Khare and Kelly L. Gibson of the Boston Regional Office.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
