On May 9, 2022, the Securities and Exchange Commission announced that it has entered into a long-term agreement to acquire, acquire and operate a global consulting firm.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in federal court in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, between September 2015 and March 2016, AECOM was unable to generate sufficient revenue to support its long-term investment plans.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in the Southern District of New York, alleges that AECOM was not able to generate sufficient revenue to meet its long-term investment targets.  The complaint further alleges that AECOM was unable to generate sufficient revenue in the first half of the year to meet those targets.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court in the Southern District of New York, charges AECOM with violations of the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The complaint charges AECOM with violations of Section 15(a) of the Exchange Act and Rule 15b2-1 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, AECOM has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 15(b) of the Exchange Act and Rules 10b-5(a) and (c) thereunder.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, has been conducted by Gaurav Kapoor and Janet Dudas of the SEC's Market Abuse Unit.  The litigation will be led by Janet Dudas.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
