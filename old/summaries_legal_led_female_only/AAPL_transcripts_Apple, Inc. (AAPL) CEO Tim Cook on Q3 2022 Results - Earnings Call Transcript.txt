On July 28, 2022, Apple Inc. ("AAPL") announced that it has agreed to pay $1.3 billion to settle a long-running legal action brought by the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.  The company also announced that it has agreed to pay a $1.3 billion civil penalty to settle a settled civil action brought by the SEC.  The company also agreed to pay a $1.3 billion penalty to settle a civil action brought by the SEC.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Apple violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Apple has agreed to settle the civil action and to pay a $1.3 billion penalty.  The settlement is subject to Court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, is being conducted by Mark Dee and supervised by Sanjay Wadhwa.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
