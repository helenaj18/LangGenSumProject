The China Advanced Construction Materials Group, Inc. (CADC) second quarter fiscal year 2014 earnings release on February 13, 2013, was announced on February 13, 2013.  The company's Board of Directors announced today that it has entered into a definitive agreement to pay a $1.4 million penalty and a $2 million civil penalty.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint, filed on February 13, 2013 in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia, alleges that China Advanced Construction Materials Group violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC's complaint charges China Advanced with violations of the antifraud provisions of Sections 17(a)(1) and (3) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and seeks permanent injunctions, disgorgement of ill-gotten gains plus interest, penalties, and injunctive relief.  The SEC's investigation was conducted by Jemming Shang and Ms. Tini of the SEC's Market Abuse Unit, and the case was supervised by Ms. Tini.  The litigation will be led by Ms. Tini.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Columbia, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
