On August 16, 2022, the SEC filed its second quarter and first half earnings results with the SEC.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Advanced Emissions Solutions, Inc. (ADES), a California-based microcap company, lost approximately $5 million in revenue in the second quarter of 2020.  According to the complaint, the company's revenue declined by 41% in the second quarter of 2021.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that, in the second quarter of 2020, the company lost approximately $3 million in revenue.  The complaint further alleges that, in the second quarter of 2021, the company reported a net loss of $2.4 million.  The SEC's complaint charges the company with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, Advanced Emissions Solutions has consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently restrains and enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 17(a)(1) and (3) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, has been conducted by Greg Emmaen, Ms. Coleman, Ms. Coleman, Ms. Coleman, and Ms. Coleman.  The litigation will be led by Ms. Coleman.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
