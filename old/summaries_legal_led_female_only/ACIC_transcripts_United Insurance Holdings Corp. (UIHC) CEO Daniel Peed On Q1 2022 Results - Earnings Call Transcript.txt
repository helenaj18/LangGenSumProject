On May 9, 2022, UPC Insurance announced that it filed for bankruptcy protection on March 31, 2022.  The SEC's complaint alleges that UPC Insurance violated Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC alleges that UPC Insurance violated Section 17(b) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder and Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint alleges that UPC Insurance violated Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act") and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, UPC Insurance has consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and orders it to pay disgorgement and prejudgment interest thereon.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  In a parallel action, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York today announced criminal charges against UPC Insurance.  The SEC's complaint charges UPC Insurance with violations of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act, Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and Rule 10b-5 thereunder and Sections 206(2) and 206(4) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940.  The complaint also charges UPC Insurance with violations of Sections 206(1) and (2) of the Investment Advisers Act and Rule 206(4)-8 thereunder, and charges UPC Insurance with violating Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act. The complaint also charges UPC with violations of Section 17(a)(1) of the Exchange Act and Rule 206(3) of the Exchange Act and Rules 206(4)-8 thereunder.
