On October 22, 2014, the SEC filed an emergency action against Acacia Research Corporation, alleging that it violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Acacia violated the antifraud provisions of Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and that Acacia violated the antifraud provision of Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act.  The SEC's complaint charges Acacia with violating the antifraud provisions of Sections 206(2) and 206(3) of the Exchange Act and Rules 206(4)-8 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Acacia has consented to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(b)(2) of the Securities Act and Section10(b) of the Exchange Act.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, Acacia has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins Acacia from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) and Rule 10b-5 of the Exchange Act and Rules 10b-5 and 16a-1 thereunder.  The settlement is subject to Court approval.  The SEC's investigation was conducted by Ashley Berkley and Jacqueline Prohm of the Market Abuse Unit, and the litigation will be led by Matt Vella.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York, the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
