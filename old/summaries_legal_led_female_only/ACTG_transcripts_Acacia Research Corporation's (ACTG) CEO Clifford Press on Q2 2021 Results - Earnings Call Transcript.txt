On August 16, 2020, the Securities and Exchange Commission announced that Acacia Research Corp. ("Acacia") announced that it filed for an initial public offering (IPO) on August 16, 2020.  The SEC's complaint alleges that Acacia, through its partnership with Starboard Value LP, raised $17.4 million in pre-public offerings in the second quarter of 2020.  According to the complaint, Acacia's valuation was significantly higher than what Starboard valued at in the second quarter of last year.  The complaint further alleges that Acacia's valuation was based on a valuation that was not fully within its control.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, charges Acacia with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the complaint, Acacia has consented to the entry of a final judgment permanently enjoining it from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. The litigation will be handled by the SEC's Office of Investor Education and Advocacy.
