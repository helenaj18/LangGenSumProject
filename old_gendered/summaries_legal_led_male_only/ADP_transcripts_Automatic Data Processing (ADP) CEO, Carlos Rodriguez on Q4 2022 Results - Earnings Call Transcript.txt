On July 27, 2020, the Securities and Exchange Commission ("SEC") announced that it has acquired ADP, Inc. ("ADP"), a microcap company, for a total of $1.5 billion.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in New York, alleges that ADP's acquisition of ADP in November 2016 generated over $1.5 billion in negative net interest income.  According to the complaint, ADP's acquisition of ADP in December 2016 generated over $1.5 billion in positive net interest income.  The complaint alleges that ADP's acquisition of ADP generated negative net interest income.  The complaint further alleges that ADP's acquisition of ADP resulted in negative net interest income.  The SEC's complaint charges ADP with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, ADP has agreed to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and to pay a civil penalty of $160,000.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's investigation was conducted by Bryan Bergin, Kevin McVeigh, and Samad Samana of the New York office.  The SEC's litigation will be handled by Mr. Bergin and Ms. Samana.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of New York and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
