The Securities and Exchange Commission today announced that Accel Entertainment, Inc. (NASDAQ:ACEL) announced record-breaking quarterly revenue and adjusted EBITDA growth.  The company also announced a $200 million share repurchase program.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Illinois, alleges that Accel violated the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, Accel has agreed to pay $20 million in disgorgement, prejudgment interest, and a civil penalty.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint charges Accel with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder. Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, Accel has consented to the entry of a final judgment that permanently enjoins it from violating the antifraud provisions of Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, and orders it to pay disgorgement of $20 million, prejudgment interest of $1.125 million, and a civil penalty of $20 million.  The settlement is subject to court approval, and Accel has agreed to pay a $20 million civil penalty.  The SEC's investigation, which is continuing, has been conducted by Chad Beynon and Greg Gibas of the SEC's Chicago Regional Office.  The litigation will be led by Derek Harmer.  The SEC appreciates the assistance of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Illinois and the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
