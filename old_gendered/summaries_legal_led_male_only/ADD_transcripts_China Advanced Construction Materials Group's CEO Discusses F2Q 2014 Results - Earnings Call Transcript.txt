The China Advanced Construction Materials Group, Inc. (CADC) second quarter fiscal year 2014 earnings release on February 13, 2013, was announced on February 13, 2013.  The company's Board of Directors announced today that it has entered into a definitive agreement to pay a $1.4 million penalty to settle charges brought by the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York.  The settlement is subject to court approval.  According to the SEC's complaint filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York on February 13, 2013, China Advanced's financial condition deteriorated significantly in the second quarter of fiscal year 2014.  According to the SEC's complaint, the company experienced a year-over-year decrease in revenue for the second quarter of fiscal year 2014.  The company's financial condition deteriorated substantially in the second quarter of fiscal year 2014 and the company's guidance for the third quarter of fiscal year 2014.  The company has a cash balance of approximately $2.4 million and short-term investments of $29 million.  As a result of the China macro-economic situation, such decrease was principally due to the slowing down of the housing market and infrastructure development.  The company's gross profit for the second quarter of fiscal year 2014 was approximately $1.4 million, a decrease of $0.8 million, and loss per share of $6.05 to $10.09.  The company's financial condition deteriorated further in the second quarter of fiscal year 2014, when it experienced a year-over-year decrease in revenues.  The company's financial condition deteriorated further during the second quarter of fiscal year 2014.  As a result of this, the company's gross profit margin decreased from 34% for the three months ended December 31, 2012 to 17% for the three months ended December 31, 2013.  The company's financial condition worsened further during the second quarter of fiscal year 2014 when it experienced a year-over-year decline in revenue.  The company's financial condition worsened further in the second quarter of fiscal year 2014 when the U.S. Attorney's Office for New York announced criminal charges against the company.  The SEC's complaint charges China Advanced with violating the antifraud provisions of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC seeks a permanent injunction, disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest, and a civil penalty.
