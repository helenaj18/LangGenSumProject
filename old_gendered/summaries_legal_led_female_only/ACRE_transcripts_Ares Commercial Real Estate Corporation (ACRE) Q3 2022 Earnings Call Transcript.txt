On November 2, 2022, the Securities and Exchange Commission ("SEC") announced that it has entered into an agreement to acquire Ares Commercial Real Estate Corporation, a real estate investment company, for a total of $192 million. The agreement is subject to court approval.  According to the SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Manhattan, the agreement is subject to court approval.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal district court in Manhattan, alleges that the agreement is subject to court approval.  In a parallel action, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of New York today announced criminal charges against Charlotte Donohoe and Tae-Sik Yoon.  The SEC's complaint, filed in U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York, alleges that Donohoe and Yoon violated Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 ("Securities Act") and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act") and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  The SEC's complaint charges Charlotte Donohoe with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and seeks permanent injunctions, disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest, and civil penalties.
