On May 2, 2018, the Securities and Exchange Commission filed an enforcement action against American Assets Trust, Inc. ("AAT"), a Portland, Oregon-based real estate investment trust, alleging that it has overstated the value of its portfolio by more than $100 million since 2014.  The SEC's complaint, filed in federal court in Portland, alleges that, in the first quarter of 2018, the company reported a loss of approximately 13% in same-store rent.  The SEC's complaint, filed in the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Oregon, alleges that, in the first quarter, the company reported a loss of $0.01 per share in same-store rent.  The complaint further alleges that, in the second quarter, the company reported a loss of approximately $0.01 per share.  The SEC's complaint charges the company with violations of Section 17(a) of the Securities Act of 1933 and Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and Rule 10b-5 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the SEC's allegations, the complaint charges the company with violating Section 17(a) of the Securities Act and Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder, and seeks permanent injunctions, disgorgement of ill-gotten gains with prejudgment interest, and a civil penalty.  The complaint also charges the company with violating Section 13(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 13b2-1 thereunder.  The complaint charges the company with violating Section 17(b) of the Exchange Act and Rules 12b-20, 13a-1, 13a-11, and 13a-13 thereunder.  Without admitting or denying the allegations in the SEC's complaint, the complaint charges the company with violating Section 15(a) of the Exchange Act and Rule 15b-5 thereunder.  The complaint also charges the company with violations of Section 17(b) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint charges the company with violations of Section 10(b) and Rule 10b-5 thereunder and seeks permanent injunctions, disgorgement with prejudgment interest, and civil penalties.  The complaint also charges the company with aiding and abetting violations of Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act.  The complaint against the complaint charges the company with violating Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act, Section 17(a) of the Exchange Act and Rules 10b-5, 13a-11, 13a-13, and 13a-14 thereunder.
